Your contribution to the task is to count the cells in the given eye image. To start the work, please follow procedures as below:

  1. Two images are provided. Image 1 is an eye image, a left figure is an image of mouse eye for cell counting, with an anterior chamber being a space surrounded by cornea, iris, and lens (same as a red shaded area in a right figure). And Image 2 is a sub-image of left figure in Image 1 as a box.
  2. First, check if Image 2 is within the anterior chamber of Image 1.
  3. If Image 2 is inside of anterior chamber in Image 1, you are ready to count the cells in that area. If not, you don’t need to count the cells (then, just type the counts to 0 the underlined section below the image).
  4. In the former case of 3), please count the cells seen as discrete bright (white) spots in the Image 2.
  5. For spots on the border of the Image 2, DO NOT COUNT THEM if the spot is covered with a yellow line in the box in Image 1 or covered with yellow and blue lines in the box in Image 1.
  6. For spots on the border of the Image 2, COUNT THEM if the spot is covered with a blue line in the box in Image 1.
  7. For spots on the border of the Image 2, DO NOT COUNT THEM if the spot is on the edge of the anterior chamber.
  8. Enter the total number of the spots in the underlined section below the Image 2.
  9. Submit the work (Thank you for your efforts!)
